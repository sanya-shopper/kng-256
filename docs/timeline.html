SHA-256 as a Mathematical Object

How we got here: a timeline of the results

The paper's chapters slice the material by discipline — history, geometry, probability, cryptanalysis, complexity. This page slices it by time. Every card below is a result the paper leans on, placed in the year it happened, in one of four running stories; the arrows mark who built on whom (solid navy), who broke what (solid wine), and where an idea jumped disciplines (dashed green).

Use it as an alternate table of contents: every card links to the section of the paper where that result does its work, with chips for the bibliography entry and the exact page of the typeset PDF. Hover a card to light up its neighborhood of influences.

Two long stories to trace top to bottom: follow the navy chain from Diffie–Hellman through Merkle–Damgård, MD5 and SHA-1 down to SHA-256 — then watch the wine arrows arrive from the left as the ancestors fall. And follow the green dashed arrows out of the mathematics lane: carries priced in 1852, random maps portrayed in 1960 and 1990, p-adic rigidity in 1994 — each becoming, decades later, an instrument pointed at this one function.

Missing arrows are not claims of independence — only the influences the paper narrates are drawn. For the full clustered bibliography with section-level backreferences, see the references page.
